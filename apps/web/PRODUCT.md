# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MotorMetrics serves people who need to understand Singapore vehicle market data without digging through raw government releases. Primary users include car buyers comparing timing and categories, industry watchers tracking make and fuel-type movement, journalists and analysts looking for evidence, and operators maintaining the site's data and content workflows.

Users usually arrive with a concrete question: what changed this month, what is happening to COE premiums, how EV and hybrid adoption is moving, which makes are gaining share, or how deregistration and vehicle-population patterns affect the market.

## Product Purpose

MotorMetrics makes LTA vehicle registration, COE, deregistration, and vehicle-population data easier to inspect, compare, and explain. Success means users can move from a broad market question to a defensible answer quickly, with charts, tables, calculators, and plain-language context that stay tied to the underlying data.

The web app should feel like a trustworthy analytics tool first, with supporting educational and editorial content that helps users understand the system rather than selling them a car or overwhelming them with raw data.

## Brand Personality

Expert, calm, precise.

The product voice should be direct and evidence-led. It can be approachable, but it should not become playful at the cost of credibility. Interface copy should make data easier to act on, not decorate obvious actions.

## Anti-references

MotorMetrics should not look like a generic SaaS dashboard: interchangeable card grids, decorative metric panels, vague insight copy, noisy iconography, and template-like analytics layouts that could fit any B2B product.

It should also avoid sales-led car dealer cues, glossy automotive promotion, and bureaucratic government-portal density. The interface should read as independent market intelligence: restrained, current, and built for repeated analysis.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the data question. Every view should make the user's likely comparison or decision path obvious.
2. Earn trust through specificity. Labels, tooltips, empty states, and chart copy should name the source, period, category, or limitation when it matters.
3. Prefer calm density. Use compact, scannable layouts where they improve analysis, but keep hierarchy clear enough for non-specialists.
4. Use colour for meaning. Reserve accent and status colour for current selection, primary actions, rank, or semantic state.
5. Keep explanations close to evidence. Educational and editorial content should connect back to the relevant chart, table, or calculation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, and interactive affordances. Do not communicate state by colour alone; pair colour with text, icons, or position. Preserve keyboard navigation and screen-reader labels for charts, filters, tables, calculators, and admin workflows.

Motion should communicate loading, navigation, or state changes, and must remain usable with reduced-motion preferences. Data views should provide empty, loading, and error states that explain what happened and what the user can do next.
